Fifteen years inside schools.

I've spent over 15 years working in schools, as a classroom teacher, curriculum leader, and Associate Principal. That means I've managed real timetables, navigated staff culture, worked with students across a full range of contexts, and led through the pressures every school leader knows well.

That experience shapes everything I bring to consulting. The approaches I share come from years of seeing what actually works in a school environment, not theory imported from outside education, but practice refined over time from inside it.

Approaches developed through years of school practice.

Over 15 years in schools, I've worked systematically across AI integration, leadership development, pedagogy, inclusion, and wellbeing, with leadership teams, classroom teachers, and administrative staff. Every approach I share has been shaped by that firsthand experience, developed through real school contexts.

I've led workshops across WA school networks and worked with both primary and secondary schools. The feedback has been consistent: practical, grounded, immediately useful.

Inclusion and equity isn't an add-on.

I lead with inclusion because AI in schools that doesn't serve every learner isn't worth doing. That means building for students with learning differences, EAL/D students, and students from disadvantaged backgrounds from the very beginning, not as an afterthought.

This shapes every session I deliver and every tool I recommend. Equity isn't a value statement. It's a design requirement.

Why I started Empowered Schools.

I kept seeing schools get sold generic PD that didn't match their context, or handed AI policies built on hype rather than reality. Leadership teams were confused and teachers were left without practical support.

Empowered Schools exists to fix that, with guidance that's honest, practical, and built by someone who understands what it actually takes to change practice in a school.
